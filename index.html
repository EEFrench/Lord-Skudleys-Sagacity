Welcome to my World!!

My name is Lord Skudley and I play Role Playing Games.

Wait, wait, before you get all excited, I’m talking about the pencil and paper, tabletop Role Playing, not the whips & chains type {not that there is anything wrong with that, occasionally whips & chains come into play in the Game, but only figuratively}.
Quite simply a Role-Playing Game is one where you pretend to be the hero {or villain, depending on the theme of the game} in an epic tale of high adventure. For example: You inherit a magical object that turns out to be an item coveted by a giant flaming eye. You must destroy this object in order to destroy the Great Evil. So, with a group of faithful companions: a warrior, a ranger, a wizard, an elf, a dwarf, and three of your childhood friends you set out on this quest. Along the way you must fight orcs, goblins, a troll, a fiery daemon, and a skulking thing.
Yes, that is a very thinly veiled version of The Lord of the Rings. But that is exactly what the world’s most well-known RPG is based upon. Truly, Dungeons & Dragons is almost entirely founded upon the works of J.R.R. Tolkien. You see, RPG’s allow you to “live” the story in a way outside the confines of the book.
Now before you get on your high horse and expound upon how evil this game is let me tell you about my journey into the “abyss” …
I grew up in the Eighties. An era based solely on the idea that we all just want to have fun. Indeed, a most shallow age, and at the same time one full of vibrant emotions. A period in time where funny glasses made you cool and Devo wore flowerpots as hats. Madonna wore cones as a bra, and Huey Lewis extolled the Hip-ness of being Square. But through it all there was one truth: D&D Is Evil!!! There was even a movie made to illustrate the follies of Role-Playing {it was called Mazes & Monsters and starred a young Tom Hanks as a gamer who went off his nut and killed someone, definitely an eye opener to the terrible end that role playing will bring you}. I knew this, I was taught this, I wasn’t sure I believed it. But my parents did, and still do for that matter. I knew a few “gamers”; they were Dorks. And coming from me that was a pretty low blow. After all I was the co-president of the Goon Gang. But my cozen played D&D, and he wasn’t a Dork, nor was he an evil satin worshiper with pentagrams carved into his arms. He was just a kid who enjoyed the Game. Still, I stayed away, because it was evil, but mainly because of the “pseudo-intellects” who tried to sound intelligent, but only succeeded in sounding like a bunch of very dorky gamers.
Then years later, many years later, I met a guy at work who played D&D. He was a smart guy, he was funny, and {most importantly}, he wasn’t a Dork, nor was he an evil satin worshiper with pentagrams carved into his arms. He was just a guy who enjoyed the Game. One day he invited me to his home to watch a session. I took my daughter and we watched. I created my first D&D character that night at the age of twenty-seven. I stayed away from the “magic” wielding characters {after all they were the truly evil aspect of the game}, and instead created a roguish villain called Lord Skudley {he later betrayed the entire party and attempted to kill the psycho dwarven cleric, but that’s another story for another time}.
I learned what a d20 was {a twenty-sided die} and what DM meant {that’s Geek for Dungeon Master. A DM is the person who runs the game and tells the story, also called a GM [Game Master]}, I bought myself my first set of gaming dice {there are seven dice in a standard d20 set: the d20, d12, d10, d8, d6, d4 and the d%[a d10 that goes from zero to ten]}. I “rolled” up characters, I learned what a “botch” was, and the difference from a “botch” and a natural twenty {a really bad roll as opposed to a really good roll}, I even learned how to properly cast a “Real” spell… You go “hommina, hommina, homMINA BANG!!!!” and roll a twenty-sided die.
I once heard how D&D taught the players to cast real spells and that the “Spell Books” contained actual spells from black magic. But when I looked into these “Real Spells” I found that the only magic they taught was how to roll some dice and consult a chart for the effect of the spell. There were no chants, no dances {well none beyond the “Hommina, hommina…” thing and a victory dance around the table as you watched the DM cry over yet another defeat as you killed his lead villain that he spent two months creating}, no lightning coming from the fingers, no mysticism, no black robes, and most importantly, there wasn’t Dork-ishness, nor was there any evil satin worshiping with pentagrams carved into our arms. The Dork-ishness did come later thou, as it must always do in the presence of the Game, but that is part of the fun.
I became a GM of my own gaming group. I taught my kids how to play. I now run these Evil Games at my own home. I have even **sigh** LARP’ed {“Live Action Role Playing”, but that’s a story for another time}.

My name is MrEd and I play RPGs…

Live hard, Game Harder,

Lord Skudley
